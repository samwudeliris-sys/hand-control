# Blind Monkey Brand Pack

## Brand Positioning

Blind Monkey is a confident, playful productivity tool for developers who want to talk to their Mac from anywhere.

It should feel:

- Fast.
- Mischievous.
- Premium.
- Developer-native.
- Clearly user-controlled.

It should not feel:

- Criminal.
- Violent.
- Stealthy.
- Like spyware or unauthorized remote control.

## Name

Primary app name:

- `Blind Monkey`

Possible subtitle:

- `Voice Remote for Mac`
- `Talk to Cursor from your phone`
- `Your iPhone mic for your Mac`

Avoid subtitles that say:

- `control any computer`
- `hack your Mac`
- `remote access without permission`

## Visual Direction

Use a monkey mascot with gangster-inspired styling that stays App Store safe:

- Sunglasses.
- Gold chain.
- Backward cap or fedora.
- Confident smirk.
- Dark background.
- Orange accent from the current Hand Control prototype.

Avoid:

- Guns.
- Drugs.
- Blood.
- Threatening poses.
- Explicit gang symbols.
- Real gang references.

## App Icon Requirements

The icon should be readable at small sizes:

- Simple monkey head silhouette.
- Sunglasses or cap as the gangster cue.
- One strong accent color.
- No small text.
- No detailed full-body character.

Recommended icon composition:

- Background: near-black radial gradient.
- Mascot: monkey head centered.
- Accessories: black sunglasses, small gold chain/collar detail, optional cap.
- Accent: orange rim glow.

Production asset sizes:

- iOS App Store: 1024x1024 PNG, no alpha.
- iOS app icons: generated from 1024 source.
- Mac app icon: `.icns` generated from 1024 source.
- Website/favicon: 512, 192, 180, 64, 32.

## Typography

iOS app:

- Use SF Pro.
- Prefer bold, compact labels.
- Keep UI copy short and direct.

Marketing:

- Headline style: confident, not edgy.
- Avoid gangster slang in core UX.

## Voice And Tone

Tone:

- Direct.
- Playful in small touches.
- Never threatening.
- Never implying unauthorized access.

Examples:

- Good: `Tap to talk`
- Good: `Mac connected`
- Good: `Recording`
- Good: `Sent to Cursor`
- Avoid: `Hijack Mac`
- Avoid: `Take over`
- Avoid: `Stealth mode`

## App Store Title And Copy

Recommended title:

`Blind Monkey`

Recommended subtitle:

`Voice Remote for Mac`

Short description:

> Use your iPhone as a voice and touch remote for your own Mac. Pair the Mac companion app, choose a Cursor window, dictate from your phone, and keep moving.

Long description outline:

1. Pair your iPhone with your Mac.
2. Swipe between Cursor windows.
3. Dictate with your iPhone microphone.
4. Send text into the selected window.
5. Use the built-in trackpad when you need to click.
6. Works locally or remotely through your secure Blind Monkey account.

Review-safe feature bullets:

- User-approved pairing.
- Visible microphone recording.
- Works only with your paired Mac.
- No Tailscale or router setup required.
- Mac companion required.

## App Store Screenshots

Screenshot set:

1. `Pair your Mac once`
   - Show Mac QR and iPhone pairing.
2. `Talk to any Cursor window`
   - Show swipeable cards.
3. `Record from your iPhone`
   - Show recording state with cancel button.
4. `Use your phone as a trackpad`
   - Show trackpad area.
5. `Works nearby or remote`
   - Show connected status, but avoid suggesting unattended access.

Screenshot captions should avoid:

- `control any Mac`
- `remote control anything`
- `bypass permissions`

## Website Copy

Hero:

> Talk to Cursor from your phone.

Subhero:

> Blind Monkey pairs your iPhone with your Mac so you can dictate, switch Cursor windows, and use a trackpad without sitting at your desk.

CTA:

> Download for iPhone
> Download Mac Companion

Trust copy:

> Blind Monkey only works with Macs you pair yourself. Recording is visible, user-started, and can be cancelled at any time.

## Privacy Copy

Use plain language:

> When you record, audio is sent to Blind Monkey for transcription. We use the transcript to send your message to your paired Mac. Raw audio is not stored by default.
